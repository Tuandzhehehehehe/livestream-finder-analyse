Tokenization Framework, a speculative model in neuroscience that proposes a novel mechanical account of surprise. Rather than viewing surprise as a simple mismatch between expectations and reality, the model suggests it is the result of neural excitation dynamics failing to rapidly converge on a stable behavioral policy. The framework raises the possibility that surprise occurs when an event recruits competing higher-order topologies, causing a transient expansion of distributed neural activity as the brain struggles to select appropriate affordances. This perspective is consistent with observed EEG components like the P300 and the engagement of the locus coeruleus-noradrenergic system, interpreting them as physical manifestations of this search for coherence.
